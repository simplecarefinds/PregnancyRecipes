Spinach Salad with Chicken

Fresh, filling, and protein-packed

A bright, satisfying salad with tender chicken, greens, and fresh toppings that makes lunch feel light but still substantial.

Prep: 12 mins Yield: 1 serving Best for: Home or work lunch

Ingredients

  • 2 cups baby spinach
  • 1 cooked chicken breast, sliced
  • 1 hard-boiled egg, halved
  • 1/2 cup cucumber slices
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 avocado, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon crumbled feta or shredded cheese, optional
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons olive oil vinaigrette

Instructions

  1. Spread the spinach over a wide plate or shallow bowl.
  2. Arrange the sliced chicken, egg, cucumber, tomatoes, and avocado in sections over the greens.
  3. Sprinkle with cheese if using.
  4. Drizzle lightly with vinaigrette just before serving so the salad stays crisp and fresh.
Chef tip: Arrange the toppings in rows or sections instead of tossing everything together. It looks prettier and gives the salad that fresh café-style feel.
Easy variation: Add strawberries, blueberries, or cooked quinoa if you want a little more texture and staying power.

Turkey & Cheese Wrap

Quick, practical, and easy to pack

This wrap is simple, balanced, and ideal for days when lunch needs to be fast, portable, and reliable.

Prep: 10 mins Yield: 1 wrap Best for: Meal prep or office lunch

Ingredients

  • 1 whole wheat wrap or tortilla
  • 3 to 4 slices fully cooked turkey
  • 1 slice pasteurized cheese
  • Handful of spinach or lettuce
  • 3 cucumber strips or slices
  • 2 to 3 tomato slices
  • 1 tablespoon hummus or cream cheese spread

Instructions

  1. Lay the wrap flat on a board and spread hummus or cream cheese evenly over the surface.
  2. Layer the turkey, cheese, greens, cucumber, and tomato through the center.
  3. Fold in the sides, then roll tightly from the bottom into a neat wrap.
  4. Slice in half and serve immediately, or wrap tightly for later.
Chef tip: Do not overfill the wrap. A thinner layer of ingredients makes it easier to roll neatly and gives cleaner slices for a prettier presentation.
Meal prep note: Pack juicy vegetables separately if making this far ahead so the wrap does not get soggy.

Quinoa & Beans Bowl

Fiber-rich and pantry-friendly

A hearty grain bowl with plant protein, easy flavor, and ingredients you can keep on hand for busy days.

Prep: 15 mins Yield: 2 servings Best for: Meal prep lunch

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup corn, optional
  • 2 tablespoons chopped parsley or cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Add the cooked quinoa and black beans to a medium bowl.
  2. Fold in the tomatoes, corn, and herbs.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and lime juice, then season lightly with salt and pepper.
  4. Toss gently until everything is evenly coated and serve chilled or at room temperature.
Chef tip: Let the quinoa cool fully before mixing so the bowl stays fluffy and not sticky.
Easy add-in: Top with avocado or shredded cheese if you want to make it more filling.

Pasta with Broccoli & Chicken

Comforting and balanced

A cozy lunch bowl with pasta, broccoli, and chicken that feels comforting while still being practical for everyday eating.

Prep: 20 mins Yield: 2 servings Best for: Warm lunch or leftovers

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked pasta
  • 1 cup cooked chicken, chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter
  • 2 tablespoons grated parmesan, optional
  • 1 small garlic clove, minced, optional
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Steam or boil the broccoli until just tender, then drain well.
  2. Warm the olive oil or butter in a skillet and add garlic if using.
  3. Add the cooked pasta, chicken, and broccoli, then toss gently until everything is heated through.
  4. Season with salt and pepper and finish with parmesan before serving.
Chef tip: Cook the broccoli until just tender and still bright green so the dish looks fresher and keeps better texture.
Serving idea: Add a squeeze of lemon at the end to brighten the whole dish without much extra effort.

Extra Chef Notes

  • For prettier lunch bowls, keep ingredients in sections instead of mixing too early.
  • Use white bowls or plates if you want the greens, tomatoes, and grains to stand out more in photos.
  • Lunches feel more satisfying when you combine protein + fiber + healthy carbs.
  • Meal prep works better when dressings and sauces are packed separately.

Easy Prep Tips

Cook once Make extra chicken and pasta so tomorrow’s lunch comes together faster.
Wash ahead Rinse spinach, tomatoes, and broccoli ahead of time for easier assembly.
Keep balance Add protein and fiber to help lunch feel more steady and filling.
Pack smart Store wraps dry and keep dressings on the side when possible.
